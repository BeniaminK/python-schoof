Detailed Description

Auxiliary functions and algorithms for rings and their elements.

The algorithms and implementations are kept as simple as possible. They are not meant for high performance computing, but for instructive purposes.

def support.rings.extended_euclidean_algorithm (   u,
  v 
)

Return a tuple (m, n, d) such that u*m + v*n = d, where d is the greatest common divisor of u and v.

The implementation does not enforce the use of integers for u and v. Thus, elements from other rings that support the divmod() operations as well as the operators //, * and - should work as well.

Note
If u and v are Polynomials, then the returned gcd polynomial is not necessarily a monic polynomial; that means the gcd may have a leading coefficient other than one. To check whether two polynomials are relatively prime, test whether the result has degree() zero.
Exceptions
ZeroDivisionErrorif one of u or v is zero.
See Also
Knuth, D. E., "The Art of Computer Programming", volume 2, second edition, p. 342
